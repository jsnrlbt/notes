## Summary

Safia Kazi argues that the global sprint to dominate the artificial intelligence sector—led by the EU, US, and China—is creating a "race to the bottom" where humanity is the primary loser. A major point of contention is the European Commission’s proposed Digital Omnibus Package, which seeks to allow data processing for AI training under the "legitimate interest" provision. This move effectively bypasses the need for individual consent, potentially weakening the robust protections previously established by the GDPR and the EU AI Act.

The article extends its critique beyond privacy, touching on the "AI bubble" where massive valuations and government-backed debt contrast sharply with a lack of profitability and diminishing returns on model scaling. Furthermore, the physical infrastructure of AI—massive data centers—is linked to severe environmental and health risks, including excessive water consumption and nitrate contamination in local water supplies. Kazi concludes that the pursuit of competitive advantage is currently steamrolling over individual rights, job security, and environmental sustainability for incremental technological gains.

## Key Takeaways

- **Erosion of Consent:** The EU's Digital Omnibus Package proposes redefining "legitimate interest" to allow AI training on personal data without seeking user consent, despite 66% of surveyed users opposing such use.
    
- **The Profitability Paradox:** Despite multi-billion dollar valuations, major players like OpenAI are not predicted to be profitable by 2030, raising concerns about an AI investment bubble.
    
- **Diminishing Returns on Scaling:** Industry experts suggest that simply adding more data and computing power (scaling) is no longer yielding breakthrough innovations toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI).
    
- **Environmental Strain:** US data centers are projected to consume 12% of the nation's electricity by 2028, with generative AI queries using significant amounts of water (approx. 500 ml per 5-50 prompts).
    
- **Public Health Risks:** High nitrate concentrations in water supplies near data centers have been linked to increased rates of cancer and miscarriages in local communities.
    
- **Resource Contradictions:** While the US seeks to lead the AI race, it simultaneously allows the sale of advanced AI chips (Nvidia H200) to top competitors like China.
    
- **Deprioritization of Humanity:** Current AI development often treats human-created content (art, stories, photos) merely as "fuel" for models rather than intellectual property deserving of compensation or protection.
